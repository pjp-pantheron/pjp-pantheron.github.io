Archdiocesan Schools for Special Ed

Mission

We believe these children, and the world around them, were brought to life by a Creator with grand and beautiful plans. So we built schools that immerse students in the wonder of their surroundings, the love of their communities, and the joy of learning that every child shares.

‍

Saint Lucy School for the Blind

With a focus on literacy and personal achievement, Saint Lucy School for students pre-k through grade 8 helps visually impaired children discover and understand their world. These students are also encouraged to attend inclusion classes at other AOPS elementary schools.

Archbishop Ryan School for the Deaf

A pre-k to grade 8 curriculum in a bilingual (English and American Sign Language) environment prepares students for a life of scholarship. Opportunity for inclusion in other AOPS elementary classes give Archbishop Ryan School for the Deaf students a strong background in communication.

‍

Our Lady of Confidence Day School

With a full curriculum of functional academics and intensive life skills training, Our Lady of Confidence helps students achieve and surpass their potential. Alumni become active, vital members of their communities.

‍

St. Katherine School

Featuring academic, vocational, occupational, and artistic classes, St. Katherine School offers a holistic curriculum. This school is uniquely positioned to teach students with multiple disabilities
